What is gaming?

The video game industry is one of the fastest growing in the World, with global profits in the gaming industry exceeding the movie industry in some cases. There's no denying that videos games are one of the most influential and diverse forms of media today, with the community growing each year.

In Australia, gaming has quickly become a pop culture staple, opening up job opportunities and unique experiences unlike any others. It has widespread appeal to several demographics and shows no signs of slowing down anytime soon.

Who's in the Industry?

Gamers

For people young and old, hardcore or casual, gaming is an exciting past-time which continues to grow in Australia. It's the gamers of the nation who fuel the industry and set the trends.

Developers

With no devs there's no games. The developers of Australia give us the games which make the industry so popular. From Fruit Ninja to BioShock Infinite, Australia has a hand in some of the world's biggest games.

eSports

eSports are rapidly growing in Australia, as players battle it out in competitive matches to win huge prizes. With the construction of an eSports arena in Sydney, eSports in Australia is only getting bigger.

History of the industry

Gaming in Australia started with the formation of Beam Software in 1978 by Alfred Milgrom and Naomi Besen. After they secured the rights, they released a game based on The Hobbit by J.R. Tolkien in 1982, which became a massive success in Europe. Following this, the late 80s and early 90s saw an influx in Australian game developers, with Micro Fort√© selling over 60,000 copies of their game, America's Cup Sailing Simulation. Following the success of these studios, the mid-late 90s saw the birth of several game developers in Australia of varying sizes. In 2000, Irrational Games opened a studio in Canberra, which contributed to two critically acclaimed games. In this same year, the Victorian Government established the 'Game Plan Initiative' to assist game developers in Australia. Unfortunately, the success would not last, as by 2018, every AAA studio opened in Australia would close for varying reasons, leaving the country with no AAA developers. Although, the recent indie boom has given rise to several Australian-based mobile game developers, with some attaining worldwide success, including Halfbrick Studios (Fruit Ninja, Jetpack Joyride), Hipster Whale (Crossy Road) and several others.
